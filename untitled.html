Raised at the feet of the Smokies, eyes constantly focused on peaks in the distance, it didn't take long for these feet and hands to begin that climb.

After contributing to several companies and working within cultures across 4 different countries, I've realized firsthand just how fast this world and it's systems are changing. I'm 99.9% sure that we can thank the internet for this, but I'm pretty convinced the accessibility of bus, boat, and balloon tickets might have played a part in this shift, as well. Regardless of what's gone down, I'm all for accepting and wielding this reality and these tools on a daily basis.

About 7 years ago and after taking this progessive oath I stumbled into photography and graphic design. Just recently it's happened again with web design and I'm thankful for these stumbles because they've allowed me to clear new paths to what's changing in this world and I won't be straying from these three anytime soon.

It's also important to note that I've always been a big dreamer. Whether it was learning how to memorize several pieces in a German song cycle or backpacking off the beaten path to find that perfect camping spot on the Great Wall, I've always wanted to bridge the gap, to go the distance. My abilities to set logic-driven goals, adapt at a moment's notice, and to analyze / push graphic and appication designs set my strides apart and have allowed me to cut through to new trails of progression, personality, and optimization.

I'm currently residing in the U.S. southeast and am taking on brand design, graphic design, web design, and web development projects. If you're looking to build something new or refine a design or application that's already in action, feel free to shoot a message my way.
