CodeWorks
Programming local startups

This program was a 12 week accelerator aimed at software engineers, coders, and hackers working on seed stage companies. It was fast-paced, highly collaborative, and very intense. Designed to help tech entrepreneurs, it helps startups find problem-solution fits, product market fits, and a scalable, repeatable business model and, as the senior design mentor, I was tasked in helping the 6 teams refine and find their face and graphic direction. I ended up advising these teams over the 12 week span working on logos, webstie mockups, slidedeck designs, social media materials, and several other components.

The Process

After working with several people in the Knoxville community and networking at several local events I came in contact with Jonathon Sexton at the Knoxville Entrepreneur Center. A few weeks later Jonathon reached out to see if I'd be interested in leading and working with 6 startups fine-tune their brands and designing all the assets (event slidedeck, pamphlets, social media graphics, banners, etc.) for the Code Works program apart. The opportunity to work with the KEC was definitely exciting and the ability to work with some many interesting projects, testing my imaginative design skills a test, was just too good to pass up!

Beginning with Research

With the group of startups focused on AppleTV app development to big data analytics, I had a lot of research and homework in front of me. Consequently, I bought several notebooks, set aside some time to talk with each of the groups, and started to take notes on where the companies where, currently, and what elements we needed to simplify or improve to bring about advancements.

After about a months worth of interations with all of the teams I was able to fill up a notebook or two full of sketchs with ideas that ranged from potential logos, webpages, mobile applications, to print materials.

These are some of the sketches that I was able to produce while working with the Code Works teams.

After working with the different groups and getting the green light, I would pivot to Illustrator or Photoshop to produce cleaner mockups. This is a small collection of the logo designs that I handed off to the teams.

Once all the fine-tuning, designing, and coding with the 6 startups was over, I moved onto the treatment of the Code Kickoff program. Here are a few sketches from the initial stages of the design process.

After talking with and working alongside Jonathon and the KEC team, I was able to produce these mockups and later pass off a great deal of material for them to use before, during, and after the Code Work program.

In the end

I feel that I am fortuntate to have met and been contacted by Jonathon and his team. The test of developing and designing 6 teams and a community program will forever be one of the more challenging tasks that I've taken on, but it also goes down as one of the most rewarding.
