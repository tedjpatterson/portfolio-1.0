The Scope

After making my way back to the U.S., getting settled, and deciding to take the daunting dive into web development waters, I was offered the chance to tread in open waters / work on a project with Maps Need Reading. This was my first solo front-end project and it was definitely a challenge. Looking back, though, I realize that I was extremely lucky to have worked with such an open minded group of people and that this project allowed me to practice simple and responsive HTML and CSS techniques. In the end, I was able to create an web interface that mirrored the bandâ€™s existing art while giving them a bold and communicative face on the web.

Periodic shifts

Going into this I knew I wanted my first site to be built with a mobile-first approach in mind. With so many people out there using phones to access information, I felt that this approach was necessary for Maps Need Reading to get the most out of personal site. Little did I know that big changes were ahead for the group and that I would get a few shots to update their branding and website, sometime down the road.

Posted below is timeline of images that portrays sketches, mockups, and photos of the band website as it progressed over time.

The initial interation of the site pulled from an upcoming album and miscelaneous art that the band had collected over the years. The band had expressed interested in sharp edges and a "lo-fi" design, so I kept fonts and the color scheme relatively basic and simple, but planned and coded responsive and modern principles into the site design.

After some time and big changes had passed, the group approached me again and we started to talk about what a revised and refined band site would look like. I previewed some trending functions/elements and design treatments that they could make use of and soon after I began sketching out a simple layout for their new site. This included a full page spread or introduction that encouraged visiters to make a choice: either stick around and learn a bit about this new band or instantly head off to listen to Maps' work, buy some merchandise, check out our Instagram account, etc.

After color schemes, font pairings, and other assorted elements had been squared away I jumped over to Photoshop to design a mockup or two. An approval was sent and the web development portion of this process fired up!

What is the team up to now?

Maps Need Reading is currently restructuring and in the process of recording new material, but they have a website that can represent them during their shift. As they approach their new destination, I hope collaborate with them again. It's always a liberating and challenging experience matching website design with artistic vision.
